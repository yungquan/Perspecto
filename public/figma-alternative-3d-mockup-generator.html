When Perspecto makes more sense than a Figma plugin

If you already live in Figma for full design systems and component libraries, plugins are great. But for a one-off mockup - a quick screenshot for a tweet, a Slack message, or a landing page update - opening Figma, finding the plugin, and configuring it is slower than just dragging your image into Perspecto directly.

What Perspecto handles that plugins often don't

Perspecto includes ready-made angle presets (Hero, Product Hunt, Side View, Top Down, Floating), a layered depth effect for a more three-dimensional look, a curved screen option, and direct PNG or HTML export - all without needing design software installed on your machine at all, which also makes it usable from a Chromebook, tablet, or any device with a browser.

No lock-in, no account

There's no account creation, no file storage, and nothing tied to a specific design tool ecosystem. Your image never leaves your browser except when you choose to export it, and your settings can be shared with a simple link if you want a teammate to use the exact same angle.
